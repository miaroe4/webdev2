Messenger

Messenger is a messaging app platform developed by Facebook, Messenger is easy to use, you can connect with Facebook friends and contacts on your phone.

Messenger can be downloaded from the Appstore and you set up your account with your phone number or Facebook. Some of the more common risks include cyberbullying, experiencing or sharing inappropriate content and chatting with strangers.

How to register:

To register you need to create a facebook account and you then use the same Facebook log in details to sign into messenger.

There are two ways to use messenger:

1. If you have a Facebook account you have messenger with it.

2. If you don't want to have a Facebook account you can deactivate your Facebook account and you will still have your messenger account.

If you wish to install the app on Android or Iphone it is available on all app stores. If you want to access messenger on your phone, tablet, etc you have to download the messenger app you can't access it through the Facebook app. You can access Messenger through Facebook on your phone when on the internet but is not recommended because it can be a bit glitchy and awkward to use.



Cautions And Warnings

If you come across possible harmful content or behavior, such as spamming, harassment, or impersonation, please report it. If we see that it violates our Community Standards, we’ll take appropriate action, which could include disabling their account or limiting their ability to send messages.

If someone’s bothering you, you can block them, and we won’t tell them they’re blocked. We’ll notify you if someone you've blocked joins group calls or chats, so you can choose to join or decline.

When you block someone, they'll no longer be able to contact you (example: send you messages, call you) on Messenger or on Facebook chat. You can also ignore a conversation, turn off notifications for a conversation, or delete a conversation.

The legal age to use the messanger app is 13
